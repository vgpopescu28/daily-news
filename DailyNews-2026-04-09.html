US-Iran Ceasefire Fragile as Israel Launches Largest Strikes Yet on Lebanon

A two-week ceasefire between the United States and Iran took effect Wednesday, contingent on Tehran reopening the Strait of Hormuz. However, the deal was immediately strained as Israel launched its largest coordinated strike of the war on Lebanon, hitting over 100 Hezbollah targets in 10 minutes in what it called "Operation Eternal Darkness." Lebanon reported at least 254 killed and over 1,165 wounded. President Trump and PM Netanyahu stated Lebanon is not covered by the Iran ceasefire, while Iran accused the US of violating the framework.

In Georgia, while Republican Clayton Fuller won Marjorie Taylor Greene's former seat, Democrats cut the GOP margin from 29 points to just 12, continuing a pattern of significant overperformance since 2025.

U.S. Policy

Trump Strengthens Section 232 Tariffs on Steel, Aluminum, and Copper

The White House announced restructured tariffs under Section 232: a flat 50% on articles made entirely of aluminum, steel, or copper, and 25% on derivative articles. The US effective tariff rate now stands at 11%, the highest since 1943, following the shift from IEEPA to Section 122 authority after a Supreme Court ruling in February.

Artemis II Crew Racing Home After Historic Lunar Flyby

NASA's Artemis II crew—Reid Wiseman, Victor Glover, Christina Koch, and Jeremy Hansen—are on their return trajectory after completing the first crewed lunar flyby in over 50 years on April 6. The crew observed micrometeor impact flashes on the Moon's surface and performed trajectory corrections as they head toward a Pacific Ocean splashdown near San Diego.

Isotopic geochemistry analysis has found no evidence that material from beyond Jupiter contributed to Earth's bulk composition, confirming the planet formed from inner Solar System material. The results also support the hypothesis that Earth's water was not delivered by comets, challenging long-held assumptions about our planet's origins.
