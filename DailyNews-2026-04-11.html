Fragile US-Iran Ceasefire Unravels as Strait of Hormuz Remains Blocked

The two-week ceasefire between the United States and Iran, brokered by Pakistan on April 8, is showing serious cracks as Iran has halted oil traffic through the Strait of Hormuz again following continued Israeli strikes on Lebanon. The waterway handles roughly 20% of global oil and gas flows, and its near-shutdown has triggered one of the most severe energy market disruptions in years. Brent crude surged above $96/barrel before tumbling over 10% on the week amid volatile swings, while hundreds of vessels remain stuck in the region.

Sources: NPR · CNBC · UN News

U.S. Policy

Policy

Trump Restructures Steel, Aluminum, and Copper Tariffs

On April 2, President Trump announced restructured Section 232 tariffs: a flat 50% on articles made entirely of aluminum, steel, or copper, and 25% on derivative products. Items made abroad with American metals face a reduced 10% rate. The US average effective tariff rate now stands at 11%, the highest since 1943.

Sources: White House · Tax Foundation
Policy

US Signs Reciprocal Trade Deals with Eight Countries

The USTR has signed reciprocal trade agreements with Argentina, Bangladesh, Cambodia, El Salvador, Guatemala, Indonesia, Malaysia, and Taiwan. Meanwhile, 60 new Section 301 investigations have been launched into forced labor practices and excess manufacturing capacity across multiple nations including China and the EU.

Sources: USTR · Yale Budget Lab
