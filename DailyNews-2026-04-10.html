Artemis II Crew Set to Splash Down Today After Historic Lunar Flyby

NASA's Artemis II mission — the first crewed flight beyond low Earth orbit since Apollo 17 in 1972 — is scheduled to splash down off San Diego at approximately 8:07 p.m. EDT today. Astronauts Reid Wiseman, Victor Glover, Christina Koch, and Canadian Space Agency astronaut Jeremy Hansen broke the record for farthest human spaceflight on April 6 as Orion swung around the far side of the Moon. The capsule will endure a six-minute communications blackout during reentry before deploying parachutes for recovery by the USS John P. Murtha.

Sources: NASA · Space.com · Netflix
